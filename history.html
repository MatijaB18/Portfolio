UNIX

The History timeline of UNIX. 

Home
Scroll
1969

Ken Thompson of Bell Labs creates the first assembly language version of an unidentified operating system for a DEC PDP-7 minicomputer.

1970

Thompson's operating system is named Unics, for Uniplexed Information and Computing Service.

1972

The first edition of the Unix Programmer's Manual, written by Thompson and Dennis Ritchie, is published.

1974

UNIX is mentioned in the Association for Computing Machinery's monthly journal. The article generates the first significant demand for Unix.

1976

Mike Lesk creates UUCP  (Unix-to-Unix Copy Program), a protocol for transferring files, e-mail, and Usenet content over a network.

1977

Unix is used on non-DEC (Digital Equipment Corporation) platforms, such as the IBM 360.

1978

Bill Joy,creates Berkeley Software Distribution (1BSD)

1982

Bill Joy co-founds Sun Microsystems in order to create the Unix-based Sun workstation.

1983

 The first Unix System V is released, which later serves as the foundation for IBM's AIX and Hewlett-Packard's HP-UX. 

1986

The initial version of Mach, a replacement kernel for BSD Unix, is created by Rick Rashid and colleagues at Carnegie Mellon University.

1987

AT&T Bell Labs and Sun Microsystems have announced plans to collaborate on the development of a system that will bring the two major Unix branches together.

1991

Linus Torvalds is the creator of Linux, an open-source operating system kernel based on Minix.

1993

Novell purchases AT&T's Unix System Laboratories and all Unix rights.

1996

The Open Group is formed when X/Open and OSF  (A Unix dev group formed by HP and IBM) merge.

2002

Version 3 of the Single Unix Specification has been published by the Open Group.
